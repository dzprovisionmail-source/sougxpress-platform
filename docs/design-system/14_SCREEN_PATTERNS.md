# 14 — SCREEN PATTERNS

> **STATUS:** PERMANENT ARCHITECTURE
> Identity: Standardized Layout Templates

---

## 1. Introduction: Consistent Interface Architecture

The Screen Patterns document defines the standardized layout templates for the most critical interfaces within Soug-XPRESS V2. By establishing these patterns, we ensure a consistent, predictable, and highly usable experience across all user roles. These templates are designed to be responsive, mobile-first, and natively supportive of Right-to-Left (RTL) languages, particularly Arabic.

---

## 2. Core Screen Templates

The following table outlines the primary screen patterns used across the platform, detailing their purpose, structural layout, and specific RTL considerations.

| Pattern Name | Purpose | Layout Structure | RTL Considerations |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Intent Gateway** | The initial entry point where users select their role (Customer, Merchant, Driver). | A clean, focused screen featuring large, distinct role selection buttons. Minimal distractions to guide the user's primary choice. | Role selection buttons and accompanying text flow from right to left. |
| **Guest Marketplace** | Allows unauthenticated users to browse the marketplace and discover local offerings. | A prominent search bar at the top, followed by category filters, and a grid or list of featured products and stores. | Search bar icons, category carousels, and product grids are mirrored for RTL reading. |
| **Authentication** | Secure entry for returning users and registration for new ones. | A streamlined form layout with clear input fields for credentials, prominent action buttons, and links for password recovery or alternative login methods. | Input fields, labels, and button text align to the right. |
| **Customer Registration** | Guides new customers through the account creation process. | A multi-step form or a single, well-organized page collecting essential details like name, phone number, and location preferences. | Form fields and progress indicators follow RTL directionality. |
| **Merchant Registration** | A comprehensive onboarding flow for new business partners. | A guided, multi-step process collecting business details, verification documents, and store setup information. Includes clear progress tracking. | Step indicators and form layouts are mirrored. |
| **Driver Registration** | Collects necessary information and documentation for delivery partners. | Similar to the Merchant flow, a structured multi-step form focusing on vehicle details, identification, and background check requirements. | Step indicators and form layouts are mirrored. |
| **Founder Dashboard** | The central command center for platform governance and observability. | A data-dense layout featuring a persistent navigation sidebar, real-time metric widgets, and actionable governance controls. | The navigation sidebar is positioned on the right. Data tables and charts flow RTL. |
| **Store Details** | Showcases a specific merchant's offerings and business information. | A prominent header image or logo, store description, operating hours, and a categorized list or grid of available products. | Store information and product listings align right. |
| **Orders** | Displays the status and history of user transactions. | A list view of active and past orders, with clear status indicators (e.g., pending, in transit, delivered) and options to view details. | Order lists and status timelines flow from right to left. |
| **Notifications** | A centralized hub for platform alerts, updates, and messages. | A chronological list of notifications, categorized by type (e.g., order updates, system alerts), with clear visual distinction for unread items. | Notification content and timestamps align right. |
| **Settings** | Allows users to manage their account preferences and configurations. | A categorized list of options (e.g., profile, security, language), leading to specific detail screens for adjustment. | Setting categories and toggle switches are mirrored for RTL. |
